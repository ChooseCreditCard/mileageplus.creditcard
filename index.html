United MileagePlus: Miles, Status & Credit Cards

MileagePlus is United Airlines’ frequent-flyer program. This page explains how MileagePlus miles are earned, how elite status works, how redemptions are structured and where credit cards fit into the picture.

Best Cards for Earning MileagePlus Miles

1. What the MileagePlus Program Is

MileagePlus is United’s loyalty and rewards program. Members collect miles on United and Star Alliance flights, as well as through on-the-ground partners and co-branded credit cards. Those miles can later be redeemed for flights, upgrades and other rewards.

The program combines:

  • A mileage currency that can be spent on United and partner flights.
  • Elite status tiers which unlock better treatment when you travel.
  • Credit card links that turn everyday spending into additional miles.

2. How MileagePlus Miles Are Earned

You can earn MileagePlus miles in several ways:

  • United flights – miles typically based on the fare you pay and your status tier.
  • Star Alliance flights – eligible partner flights credited to your MileagePlus account.
  • Co-branded credit cards – miles per unit of currency spent, often with bonus categories.
  • Hotels, car rentals and shopping – partner activity where you add your MileagePlus number.
  • Promotions – seasonal or targeted offers that provide extra miles on specific routes or actions.

Over time, many members find that a large share of their miles comes from credit card spend and partner activity rather than from flights alone.

3. How Credit Cards Boost MileagePlus Earnings

MileagePlus-linked credit cards let you earn miles on non-flight purchases and may add practical travel perks. Depending on your country, typical card features include:

  • Base earn rates on all purchases, plus elevated earn rates on United or travel-related spend.
  • Welcome bonuses when you reach a required minimum spend in the first few months.
  • Travel benefits such as priority boarding, free checked bags or airport lounge access.
  • Status support where spending can help with elite qualification metrics in some markets.

Used consistently, the right MileagePlus card can make a noticeable difference to how quickly your mileage balance grows between trips.

4. Redemption Categories in MileagePlus

MileagePlus miles can be redeemed for:

  • Award flights on United – short- and long-haul itineraries paid with miles plus taxes.
  • Star Alliance partner flights – redemptions that use alliance partners to reach more destinations.
  • Seat upgrades – moving from economy to higher cabins on some tickets and routes.
  • Other rewards – such as hotels, cars or experiences in certain markets.

As with most airline programs, many travelers focus their miles on flights and upgrades, where the potential value per mile is usually strongest.

5. MileagePlus Elite Status Tiers

MileagePlus offers multiple elite levels. Names and qualification metrics can change over time, but the typical structure looks like this:

  • Member – entry level with standard earning and redemption.
  • Premier Silver – base elite tier with some priority services and limited upgrades.
  • Premier Gold – stronger upgrade priority and additional travel benefits.
  • Premier Platinum – higher upgrade priority and more generous allowances.
  • Premier 1K – top published tier with the widest range of perks and recognition.

Certain co-branded cards can accelerate your path toward these tiers by providing qualifying boosts or waivers, depending on the market and product.
